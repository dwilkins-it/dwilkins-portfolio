Organizational Design Operating Model
Designing a Stewardship-Based Operating Model for a Global IT Team

2025 · 11-person cross-functional team · 3 regions

Situation

Leadership directed a "back to basics" effort. I'd already been pushing for a return to core IT function — defined ownership, governed change, operational discipline. Used the directive as the opening to formalize and deploy the operating model I'd been advocating for.

Approach

  • Three-bucket structure: Operating Rhythm, Individual Enhancements, Documentation — every team member accountable across all three
  • Every platform mapped to a named steward with defined accountability
  • Governed enhancement pipeline with CAB review and change ticket requirements
  • Structured 1:1 framework with pitch templates for enhancements and documentation staging
  • Measurement tied directly to bucket performance — not abstract ratings

Outcomes

  • ✔ Complete operating model designed, documented, and deployed
  • ✔ All improvement work flows through a governed, traceable pipeline
  • ✔ 1:1s shifted from status updates to development conversations
  • ✔ Leadership has real-time visibility without asking
  • ✔ Framework designed to survive leadership transitions

Governance Change Management
Building a Change Management Framework for a Lean IT Team

2025–2026 · v5.1 · ITIL-aligned · SOX / SOC 2 / ISO compliant

Situation

Publicly traded company with SOX and ISO obligations, no formalized change management for IT operations. Changes lacked traceability, approval trails were inconsistent, and separation of duties wasn't enforced.

Approach

  • Two change types (Normal + Emergency) — deliberately held back Standard type until team habits are proven
  • Dual approval gates with structural SOD enforcement — not policy-based, architecture-based
  • 7-question risk decision tree replacing rigid risk matrices — planning depth scales to actual risk
  • Full ITSM automation: lifecycle gates, calendar integration, Slack notifications, compliance DMs to Technology Compliance Director
  • Reference example baked into the policy as the quality bar for every ticket

Outcomes

  • ✔ Complete change management policy — actively iterated to v5.1
  • ✔ Full ITSM automation behind the lifecycle
  • ✔ SOX, SOC 2, and ISO audit-ready from day one
  • ✔ Separation of duties enforced structurally
  • ✔ Team adoption without resistance
M&A Integration 3 Acquisitions

Automation at Scale $135K+ Saved
From 0% to 60% Ticket Automation

2022–2024 · 900+ tickets/month

Situation

Entirely manual service desk. Every request required human processing. Volume outpacing capacity during growth from ~1,100 to 1,600+ employees.

Approach

  • Approval workflows: Automated chains tied to manager hierarchies and role-based Okta provisioning
  • Lifecycle automation: End-to-end JML from onboarding bot to termination device lock
  • AI routing: Models for ticket categorization and intelligent assignment
  • Self-service: 150+ catalog items and hiring manager access portals

Outcomes

  • ✔ 60% ticket automation rate
  • ✔ ~2,700 manual hours eliminated annually
  • ✔ $135K+ annual labor cost avoidance
  • ✔ Team capacity freed for strategic work

Process Transformation Global
Global Employee Lifecycle Automation

2023–2025 · 7 lifecycle events

Situation

Fragmented lifecycle management. Manual HR/IT coordination. Offboarding gaps. Mid-lifecycle events (transfers, title changes) not tracked at all.

Approach

  • Rebuilt onboarding Slack bot with real-time status and hiring manager self-service
  • Automated data ingestion pipeline with HR for real-time events
  • Expanded to 7 additional events: transfers, manager/title/location changes, rehires, conversions
  • Automated termination: device lock, retrieval, ownership handoff

Outcomes

  • ✔ 40% reduction in Day 1 onboarding delays
  • ✔ 7 additional lifecycle events automated
  • ✔ Self-service for hiring managers
  • ✔ Eliminated manual coordination for routine events
